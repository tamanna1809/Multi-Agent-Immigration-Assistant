=== UNITED STATES VISA TYPES ===

H-1B VISA (Specialty Occupation Worker):
Purpose: Employment in specialty occupation requiring a bachelor's degree or higher equivalent
Eligibility Requirements:
- Job offer from a US employer willing to act as sponsor
- Position must qualify as a specialty occupation (IT, engineering, finance, accounting, architecture, law, medicine, etc.)
- Minimum bachelor's degree or equivalent in the specific specialty
- Employer must file Form I-129 petition with USCIS
- Employer must pay the prevailing wage for the occupation
Annual Cap: 65,000 per fiscal year (additional 20,000 for US master's degree holders)
Lottery: Applications in April for October 1 start. If oversubscribed, random lottery selection
Duration: Initial 3 years, extendable to 6 years. Extensions beyond 6 years possible if green card process initiated
Processing Time: 3-6 months standard; 15 business days with Premium Processing ($2,805 fee)
Key Risk Factors:
- Cap lottery — only ~26% of petitions are selected
- Specialty occupation definition scrutinized by USCIS
- Wage level requirements (Level I vs Level II wages)
- Site visits by USCIS officers
- H-1B transfer requires new petition if changing employers
Required Documents: Valid passport, Form I-129 (employer files), educational certificates and transcripts, resume/CV, employment offer letter, DS-160 confirmation, fee receipt, 2x2 photo (white background), previous US visas if applicable

F-1 VISA (Student):
Purpose: Full-time academic study at an accredited US institution
Eligibility Requirements:
- Acceptance at a SEVP-certified (Student and Exchange Visitor Program) school
- Enrollment in a full course of study
- Sufficient financial support to cover tuition and living expenses
- Strong ties to home country (intent to return after studies)
- Proficiency in English (TOEFL/IELTS)
Processing Time: 3-5 weeks
Duration: Duration of Status (D/S) — as long as enrolled and maintaining status
Key Risk Factors:
- Must demonstrate non-immigrant intent (ties to home country)
- Financial sufficiency proof critical
- Nigerian, Indian, Chinese students face higher scrutiny
- Attending low-ranked or for-profit schools raises flags
Required Documents: Valid passport, Form I-20 from school, SEVIS fee receipt (Form I-901), DS-160 confirmation, visa fee receipt, financial evidence (bank statements 3 months minimum $10,000+ for first year), acceptance letter, academic transcripts, English proficiency scores, evidence of ties to home country

O-1 VISA (Extraordinary Ability):
Purpose: Individuals with extraordinary ability in sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics
Eligibility: Must demonstrate sustained national or international acclaim via:
- Major awards (Oscars, Nobel, Olympic medals)
- Published articles in major media or professional journals
- Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
- High salary relative to peers
- Participation as judge of others' work
- Critical/essential role in distinguished organizations
Processing Time: 2-3 months standard; premium processing available
Duration: Up to 3 years, extendable in 1-year increments
Risk Factors: High evidence bar, subjective USCIS evaluation

B-1/B-2 VISA (Business/Tourism):
Purpose: Temporary business visits (B-1) or tourism/pleasure (B-2)
Duration: Up to 6 months
Requirements: Valid passport, demonstration of temporary visit intent, ties to home country, sufficient funds
Risk Factors: Overstay history, prior refusals, weak ties to home country

EB-5 VISA (Immigrant Investor):
Purpose: Permanent residence through investment
Minimum Investment: $1,050,000 standard; $800,000 in Targeted Employment Area
Job Creation: Must create or preserve 10 full-time jobs for US workers
Processing Time: 2-5 years
Risk: Investment at risk, complex requirements

=== CANADA VISA TYPES ===

Express Entry — Federal Skilled Worker Program:
Purpose: Economic immigration for skilled foreign workers seeking permanent residence
Eligibility: Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score based on:
- Age (maximum points at 20-29 years)
- Education (Canadian or foreign equivalent assessed by ECA)
- Language skills (IELTS for English, TEF for French) — CLB 7 minimum
- Work experience (minimum 1 year skilled work in NOC 0, A, or B)
- Adaptability factors (Canadian education, prior work, family in Canada)
Process: Create Express Entry profile → enter pool → receive Invitation to Apply (ITA) if score above cutoff → apply for PR within 60 days of ITA
CRS Cutoff Scores: Typically 470-520, fluctuates with draw frequency
Processing Time: 6 months from ITA to PR
Risk Factors:
- CRS score may not meet cutoff for general draw
- ECA takes 2-3 months
- Language scores must meet minimums
- Misrepresentation leads to permanent ban
Required Documents: Valid passport, IELTS/CELPIP results, Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) from WES or equivalent, employment reference letters (NOC description, salary, duties, supervisor contact), police clearance certificates from every country lived in 6+ months, medical examination from designated physician, proof of funds (varies by family size — single applicant minimum CAD $13,310), photographs meeting IRCC specifications

Intracompany Transfer (ICT) — Canada:
Purpose: Transfer within a multinational company to Canada
Requirements: Employed by company outside Canada for minimum 1 year within last 3 years, position must be managerial, executive, or specialized knowledge
Processing Time: 2-4 weeks at port of entry

Canadian Study Permit:
Purpose: Full-time study at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
Requirements: Acceptance letter from DLI, proof of financial support (CAD $10,000 per year minimum plus tuition), intent to leave after studies
Processing Time: 4-8 weeks (some countries have Student Direct Stream — 20 days)
Student Direct Stream Countries: Antigua and Barbuda, Brazil, China, Colombia, Costa Rica, India, Morocco, Pakistan, Peru, Philippines, Senegal, Trinidad and Tobago, Vietnam

=== UNITED KINGDOM VISA TYPES ===

Skilled Worker Visa (UK):
Purpose: Work in the UK with an approved employer
Requirements:
- Job offer from UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) licensed sponsor
- Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) from employer
- Job at appropriate skill level (RQF Level 3 or above)
- Minimum salary: £26,200 per year OR the going rate for the occupation (whichever is higher)
- English language requirement: B1 level (CEFR)
Points Required: 70 points (mandatory: job offer 20pts + salary threshold 20pts + English 10pts + remaining 20pts from tradeable characteristics)
Processing Time: 3 weeks from outside UK
Duration: Up to 5 years, extendable
Settlement: Eligible for Indefinite Leave to Remain after 5 years
Risk Factors: Sponsor license status, salary below threshold, English test validity

UK Student Visa:
Purpose: Study at licensed student sponsor institution
Requirements: Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from institution, English language B2 level, financial requirements (£1,334/month in London; £1,023/month outside London for up to 9 months, plus first-year tuition fees)
Processing Time: 3 weeks
ATAS: Academic Technology Approval Scheme required for certain sensitive subjects (physics, chemistry, advanced mathematics, certain engineering disciplines)

=== DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS DETAILED ===

H-1B Complete Document List:
1. Valid passport with minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay
2. Form I-129 Petition (filed by employer with USCIS — not filed by applicant)
3. I-129 approval notice from USCIS
4. Educational certificates (bachelor's degree minimum)
5. Official academic transcripts (sealed)
6. Resume/CV detailing work history
7. Employment offer letter (position, salary, start date, duration)
8. DS-160 Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application confirmation page
9. MRV visa application fee receipt (currently $185)
10. Passport-style photo (2x2 inches, white background, taken within 6 months)
11. Previous US visas (if any, even if expired)
12. Previous US immigration documents (I-20, EAD, etc. if applicable)
13. Employer's financial documents showing ability to pay wages (optional but helpful)
14. Specialty occupation evidence (job description, industry standards)

F-1 Complete Document List:
1. Valid passport
2. Form I-20 from SEVP-certified school (must be signed by DSO)
3. SEVIS fee payment receipt (Form I-901, currently $350)
4. DS-160 confirmation page
5. MRV visa fee receipt ($185)
6. Photo meeting requirements
7. Financial evidence: bank statements showing 3+ months history, scholarship letters, sponsor affidavit of support
8. Acceptance/enrollment letter from institution
9. Academic transcripts (all previous institutions, translated to English)
10. English proficiency test scores (TOEFL iBT minimum 80, IELTS minimum 6.0 — varies by school)
11. Ties to home country: property deed, family documents, employment letter, business ownership proof
12. SAT/ACT scores if applying to undergraduate program (some consulates request)

Canada Express Entry Documents:
1. Valid passport (all passports held in last 10 years)
2. Language test results (IELTS General Training: minimum 6 in all bands for CLB 7; or CELPIP; or TEF for French)
3. Educational Credential Assessment from WES, ICAS, or other approved body (3 months processing)
4. Employment reference letters (one per qualifying job, on company letterhead, signed, detailing: position, start/end dates, hours per week, salary, duties, supervisor name and contact)
5. Police clearance certificates from every country lived in 6+ months since age 18
6. Medical examination results from IRCC designated physician (valid 12 months)
7. Proof of funds bank letter showing sufficient settlement funds
8. Birth certificate
9. Marriage certificate or common-law declaration if applicable
10. Photos (35mm x 45mm, white background)

=== RISK FACTORS AND RED FLAGS ===

Critical Red Flags (High Impact on Approval):
1. Previous visa rejection or overstay: Must be disclosed. H-1B officers cross-reference. Previous US overstay can result in 3-year or 10-year bar.
2. Criminal record: Any conviction, including minor offenses, must be disclosed. Drug offenses particularly serious. DUI can cause UK visa refusal.
3. Misrepresentation: Any false statement, even minor, can lead to permanent inadmissibility.
4. Incomplete or inconsistent application: Gaps in employment/education history unexplained.
5. No ties to home country: For non-immigrant visas, must demonstrate reasons to return.
6. Insufficient financial evidence: Bank statements showing recent large deposits (suspicious), insufficient funds.
7. Travel to certain countries: Travel to countries under sanctions or travel warnings can raise scrutiny.

Medium Risk Factors:
1. First-time traveler to target country
2. Recent job change or unemployment gap
3. Self-employment without clear documentation
4. Applying near expiry of current status
5. Weak English skills
6. Low-ranked educational institutions
7. Age extremes for certain visa types

Low Risk Factors (Positive Indicators):
1. Strong prior travel history with full compliance
2. Previous visa approvals (same or other countries)
3. Stable long-term employment
4. Clear financial stability
5. Family ties in home country (spouse, children, parents)
6. Property ownership in home country
7. High salary and prominent employer

=== PROCESSING TIMES 2024-2025 ===
US H-1B: 3-6 months standard, 15 business days premium processing
US F-1: 2-5 weeks (varies heavily by country and consulate)
US O-1: 2-3 months standard
US B-1/B-2: 2-8 weeks (varies by consulate)
Canada Express Entry: 6 months from ITA
Canada Study Permit: 4-8 weeks standard, 20 days via Student Direct Stream
UK Skilled Worker: 3 weeks from outside UK
UK Student: 3 weeks

=== COMMON REASONS FOR REJECTION ===
1. Failure to demonstrate non-immigrant intent
2. Insufficient financial evidence
3. Incomplete application or missing documents
4. Failed to disclose prior immigration violations
5. Suspicious travel patterns
6. Inconsistencies between application and supporting documents
7. Criminal inadmissibility
8. Medical inadmissibility
9. Prior overstay or violation
10. Security grounds
